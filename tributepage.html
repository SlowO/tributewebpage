Ehud Manor

Here's a time line of Ehud Manor's life:

  • 1941 - Born in Binyamina as Ehud Weiner.
  • 1959 - Graduated from the Hebrew Reali School in Haifa.
  • 1960s - Ehud Manor began working for Israel radio as a musical editor. He changed his surname to Manor as it was customary at the time for radio announcers to adopt Hebrew names.
  • 1975 - Wrote the lyrics to Israeli Eurovision Song Contest entry "At Va'Ani (You and Me)" with its singer Shlomo Artzi."
  • 1977 - Had the television musical show "Eretz Tropit Yafa" (lit. "A Beautiful Tropical Country").
  • 1978 - Wrote the lyrics to winning Israeli Eurovision Song Contest entry "Abanibi".
  • 1982 - Had the television show "Red River Valley" in which he translated famous country songs.
  • 1998 - Awarded the Israel Prize for Hebrew song.
  • Early 2000s - Diagnosed with lung cancer which he eventually recovered from, but the cancer treatment weakened his heart.
  • 2005 - Died from cardiac arrest at the aged of 63.

"For the past 30 years, he has expressed our mood through the hundreds of songs he has written together with the finest composers. The man who declared that he had no other country is the laureate of the Israel Prize."

-- Israel Prize's committee
